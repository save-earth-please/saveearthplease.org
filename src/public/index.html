Guidelines with facts and figures on how to use a personal co2 emissions budget to contribute to keeping Earth mostly hospitable. Let's be heroes.

Save Earth, please:

  • Try to budget for only 1.6 tonnes of carbon dioxide emissions or less per year. This is the amount we have available - split fairly between everyone on Earth - to keep the planet mostly hospitable for our foreseeable future.
  • Avoid flying. Flying economy Europe to USA emits about 0.5 to 1 tonnes per passenger, Europe to Australia about 2 tonnes per passenger - ICAO.
  • Avoid plastic, especially single-use plastic. Using 0.5 tonnes in a year of plastic produces over 0.9 tonnes of carbon dioxide emissions - CIEL.
  • Switch to energy from renewable sources. Generating household-sized energy of between 5000 and 10000 kilowatthours from coal for a year requires produces about 4.5 to 9.2 tonnes of carbon dioxide - EIA. Renewables, in comparison, do not produce these emissions.
  • Avoid eating meat
  • Consume mainly food and products from your local region
  • Avoid going on a cruise ship
  • Offset outsized greenhouse gas emissions
  • Minimise your use of plastic
  • Minimise the amount of content you stream online
  • Use a bike or walk to nearby locations
  • Engage in your local community and politics to work towards solutions that reduce emissions at scale

What is a fair amount of emissions to produce in a year?

The Paris Agreement aims for a 40% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions compared to 1990 levels. That would mean a target of about 12.4 gigatons of carbon dioxide emissions, for example. (In 2018, over 33.1 gigatons of carbon dioxide were emitted - IEA). This means there is a personal carbon dioxide budget of about 1.6 tonnes for each person on Earth. At the moment, 4.3 tonnes is produced for each person.

Am I allowed to do anything?

The above suggestions are not strict rules about what you can and cannot do. The main point is to recognise that we each have a fair share of the emissions and we should budget that well so that we can live comfortably now and for many years into the future. By reducing emissions now, we reduce the severity of the climate crisis for the already turbulent decades ahead of us. Please consider engaging in emission offset programs if you produce over your fair share of 1.6 tonnes of carbon dioxide emissions in a year. Spending time with friends, explore nearby parks and cultural points of interests, read, do something creative. There are plenty of ways to enjoy life on Earth while having a minimal impact on its ecosystems.

Isn't this the responsibility of big industrial polluters?

Avoiding catastrophic climate change is everyone's responsibility, I believe. It requires a heroic, global team effort to avoid a future for Earth that is looking increasingly dire. Of course this includes the big polluters pulling their weight. However, when we consumers purchase indirectly or directly from the big polluters, we are pulling at the supply chain that keeps the big polluters in business. In this way, their failure to properly address the climate crisis is also our failure. Let's identify the ways we are doing this, then put our money and time into activities that will save Earth instead.

About this project

This site was created after I realised that I was dramatically overspending my fair share of carbon emissions despite there being no obvious warning signs. Prior to informing myself using a carbon dioxide emissions calculator, I thought I was easily offsetting a flight abroad to see my family by eating a mostly vegan diet. The emissions calculator revealed that a year of eating vegan food - while reducing a helpful 0.7 tonnes of emissions - did not even come close to offsetting my flights which emit somewhere between about 2 and 10 tonnes of carbon dioxide (depending in which calculation is used). Now that I know this, I can work towards reducing and offsetting these outsized emissions to live within my budget of 1.6 tonnes of carbon dioxide emissions per year. It took coincidentally listening to a couple of talks on climate change, followed by active research to realise that I was overspending my personal emissions budget. With saveearthplease.org, I would like to document my findings so it is easy for everyone to recognise their own current emissions spending and then create personal emission budgets to make the most out of life while helping slow down the climate crisis we find ourselves in.
